modulo

Do you remember remainders and stuff from high school? Those are formally known as mods.

As with all of these tutorials, if I make a side remark and it doesn’t click with you, just ignore it.

Let’s define mods formally it’s not even formal whoever keeps doing the strikethrough stoppit. A mod is the remainder of division, so we can write something like

7≡10mod37≡10mod3
cos both of them leave a remainder of 11 when divided by 3. We can define a least residue to be the smallest “positive” mod possible. So for instance, the least residue of 100mod7100mod7 is 2.

Ex. Find the least residue of 1010010100 for each of the mods 3,5,7,9,11

Note that a≡bmodma≡bmodm means that m|a−bm|a−b where the || simply means “is a factor of”.

Thus prove that ≡≡ is an equivalence relation, in other words, that the following three hold under any fixed mod:

Equivalence relations

a≡aa≡a
a≡b⟹b≡aa≡b⟹b≡a
a≡b,b≡c⟹a≡ca≡b,b≡c⟹a≡c

Time for some other mod facts:

Useful lemma

If a≡ca≡c and b≡db≡d, then

a+b≡c+d,ab≡cda+b≡c+d,ab≡cd

So what use are mods? In number theory, some questions are much simpler under a mod because under a mod, you don’t need to check every number anymore. Consider, for instance, the equation:

x2+4z+10=y2x2+4z+10=y2

It’s got 3 whole variables! This makes it pretty difficult to solve using regular means. However, if you look at the equation under a mod 4 lens, it looks easier:

x2+2≡y2x2+2≡y2

Now, what can x2x2 be mod 4? There are only four cases to check (by useful lemma): 02,12,22,3202,12,22,32 which are 0,1,0,10,1,0,1 mod 4. Thus x2+2x2+2, the LHS, must be 22 or 33 mod 4. Similarly, the LHS must be 00 or 11, and thus since the LHS and RHS never match mod 4, they cannot be the same.

It’d have been an oversight to write a mods post without Fermat’s Little Theorem:

Fermat’s Little Theorem

ap−1≡1modpap−1≡1modp
when p/|ap⧸|a

So when pp isn’t a factor of aa, we get this cool result! (what happens when pp is a factor of aa?)

There is actually a stronger version of this which states aϕ(n)≡1modnaϕ(n)≡1modn when gcd(a,n)=1gcd(a,n)=1 where ϕϕ is the euler totient function, but unless you understood that, it’s probably irrelevant.

Well, what does 1212 really mean? No, it does not mean “zero point five”. Not here in dreamland, anyway. 1212 means “the number which becomes 1 when doubled”. But when you double 66, you get 12≡112≡1. Try and define square roots and other fractions and stuff in the same way! Even better, prove that they behave as you’d expect — e.g

for i=1,2,3,…,ni=1,2,3,…,n then this is solvable if the mimis pairwise have no common factors. Normally you’d use this to show the existence of a solution without explicitly finding it.

questions

These questions are unethically sourced but are good training and totally legal. pp always denotes a prime.

  1. Solve
    m3n=n2m+mn+m+nm3n=n2m+mn+m+n
    in integers.
  2. Solve 2a+3b=c22a+3b=c2 in integers (you may assume Catalan’s conjecture, although it’s unnecessary but the question ends up becoming harder I think)
  3. When can you divide in a mod? Is division always uniquely defined? In particular, consider the set
    {0a,1a,2a,3a,…,(p−1)a}modp{0a,1a,2a,3a,…,(p−1)a}modp
    What is this set?
  4. If you have mm and nn dollar coins, how many different totals can’t you make? For instance, if you had 4 and 5 dollar coins, you can’t make 1,2,3,6,7,11 for a total of 6.
  5. Prove that
    (p−1)!≡−1modp(p−1)!≡−1modp
    for all primes pp, where !! is a factorial.
  6. What is the smallest number of cubes you need such that their sum is 2002200220022002?
  7. On a circle, Alina draws 2019 chords, the endpoints of which are all different. Consider all 4038 endpoints and intersections of at least two chords.
    Alina labels each of these points. 2019 endpoints are marked 0, and the other 2019 are marked 1. Each intersection of chords is labelled with an arbitrary integer. For each segment (part of a chord which ends in two labelled points, and has none in its interior) Alina writes the sum of the two endpoints of the segment. Suppose that these sums are the numbers 0,1,2,…,N0,1,2,…,N in some order. Prove that there exists a segment such that the difference of the two numbers on the ends is divisible by 3.
  8. Find all pairs of primes (p,q)(p,q) such that pq|5p+5qpq|5p+5q
  9. Prove that a1=a,an+1=aana1=a,an+1=aan is eventually constant under any mod.
  10. Prove that pp divides Fp−1FpFp+1Fp−1FpFp+1 where FF are the Fibonacci numbers.
