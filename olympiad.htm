“detailed” rundown of posts

all dot points are valid unless otherwise stated:

  • all posts have 10 exercise questions
  • all posts are roughly 1.5*
  • all posts are suitable for one with no prior knowledge of the topic, but competence in mathematics
basic

methods of proof 1* skims methods of proof: direct proof, induction, contradiction and contrapositive. post difficult is 1*, questions range from 0* to 2*; even spread

algebra

functional equations very basics of functional equations. The problems range from 1* to around 3*
AM-GM introducing the very basics of inequalities. Questions range from 1* to around 5.5*, with a gradual increase in difficulty
polynomials introduces some polynomial techniques; questions range from 1.5* to 4.5*
sequences introduces what sequences are, questions start 1* but very quickly go to 5*. Not suitable for people new to analysis and/or algebra
strange functional equations no tutorial, assumes you know functional equations. questions start at 1* and immediately shoot up to 4*, staying at around 4-5*.
analysis of sequences the post material is 2.5* and covers recurrence relations as well as boundedness and stuff. questions are 3*-4*

combinatorics

bijections perhaps 2* to really wrap your head around, the questions are not suitable for anybody who hasn’t done basic combinatorics. The questions slowly ramp up from 2* to around 5*
invariants questions start at 1.5* and get steadily harder up til around 5.5*
combinatorial games doesn’t have the standard 10 question layout introduces combinatorial game theory; questions range from 1* to 2.5* and then it finishes with a 4.5* question.
combinatorial induction this assumes you get the basics of induction, and then asks questions on a gentle slope from 1* to 5*
combinatorial colourings no tutorial, throws you into questions to get the hang of things. the questions go from 0.5* gently up to about 4.5* for question 9, and I’m not sure who put it there but question 10 is 6.5* or so

geometry

basic geometry very basics of geometry. start here if you’re new to olympiad geo questions go from around 0.5* to 2*, maybe 2.5*
general geo a short article detailing how to get good at geo, but has no examples/questions
circle geo differently formatted post; starts 0.5* and slowly and steadily builds up a repertoire of skills. To get even decent at geo, most of the lemmas (especially the first few) are must-knows. The last few geos end up being somewhat hard (4*) so don’t panic too much.
directed angles if you keep getting 6/7 (marks) for geo questions and wanna level up to 7/7, read this post. Directed angles aren’t a “new technique” that you should learn, they’re just a way to rewrite all your old proofs (without actually doing much) to work. Questions go from 1* and slowly and steadily creep up to around 5.5*
geometric constructions another very useful post on olympiad geometry, but only for pushing from decent to good. questions go from 2* or so to 5+*; difficulty for these questions is hard to judge
reverse reconstruction this post details yet another useful geometry technique. Come to think of it, every post details a useful technique. Like, basically every one. Anyways, questions from 0.5* to 5*
inversion for people who want to learn inversion. inversion is very powerful, but much more advanced than circle geo and directed angles. post material is 2*, and the problems range from 2* to around 4*, question 9 to 5.5*, question 10

number theory

diophantine equations really, 1* to 7+*. This post is a conglomeration of random techniques for you to look at, and the questions are well worth attempting until they get hard. Doesn’t follow the ten question format. (well, it’s 10+1 cos the 1 is kinda a joke but whatever).
divisibilities what it sounds like. Questions are a lot easier than the standard fare, starting at 1* and very slowly going up to around 2.5*, and then the last two questions are around the 3-4* mark.
mods modulo arithmetic, which is one of the most fundamental things in olympiad number theory. The questions start at around 1.5* and stay at around 1.5* until around question 7 and then they get very hard (5+*).
cyclotomic polynomials quickly skims the most important facts about cyclotomic polynomials which are useful in very specific questions and are otherwise useless. All in all, a very hard post. The questions start at around 1.5-2* and have a steep difficulty curve (around 4* at question 3) all the way up to 5.5* or so.

hybrids

combi geo 1 an easy post detailing, well, combinatorial geometry. Questions start 0* and go up to 3*
combi geo 2 a continuation of the last post. Questions only. Questions start at 2.5* and rocket up, and stay at, around 5*, perhaps skimming 5.5*
integer functions I’m not sure why, but the example questions seem harder than most of the “actual” questions. The first half of the questions are mostly 1.5-2* but the second half quickly climbs to 4.5-5+*
infinite integer sequences skims some basics about infinite integer sequences, and advantages over normal sequences. problems start 2* and ramp up fairly quickly to 5*+
