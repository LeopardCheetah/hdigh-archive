questions

Lemma* 1 angles in a triangle

Prove ∠ACB=∠ADB∠ACB=∠ADB and ∠ABD+∠ACD=180∘∠ABD+∠ACD=180∘ (set up and prove the converse, too, if you’re feeling up to it).

*I hope you don’t mind me calling these lemmas

Lemma 2 power of a point

Prove △PAC∼△PDB△PAC∼△PDB (where ∼∼ indicates similarity).

Thus show that PA⋅PB=PC⋅PD=PO2−r2PA⋅PB=PC⋅PD=PO2−r2, where rr is the radius of the circle.

What happens if PP lies inside the circle? (make the quantity negative).

Let’s denote this quantity PO2−r2PO2−r2 as PΓ(P)PΓ(P), or the power of PP with respect to the circle, ΓΓ.

Lemma 3 radical axis

Given two circles ω1ω1 and ω2ω2, consider the locus of points PP such that

Pω1(P)=Pω2(P)Pω1(P)=Pω2(P)
Prove that this locus is a line.

Hence show that for any two non-intersecting circles, the four midpoints of the common tangents are collinear.

Furthermore prove that for any three pairwise intersecting circles, the three lines through the intersection points of each pair of circles* are concurrent.
*see diagram

Lemma 4 harmonic quadrilateral aka poles and polars

Prove that in a cyclic quadrilateral, AC⋅BD=AD⋅BCAC⋅BD=AD⋅BC if and only if the tangents at AA and BB lie on CDCD.

Thus prove that if the tangents at AA and BB lie on CDCD, the tangents at CC and DD lie on ABAB.

ABAB is called the polar of PP and CDCD the polar of QQ.

Thus if PP lies on the polar of QQ, then QQ lies on the polar of PP

Bonus exercise: find a consistent way of characterising the polar of points inside the circle.

Lemma 5 charles’ point

Prove that if in a cyclic quad, if ∠BAM=∠MAC∠BAM=∠MAC then BM=MCBM=MC. Also prove that MI=MB=MCMI=MB=MC where II is the incenter of ABCABC.

Lemma 6 incyclic quadrilateral

Prove that a convex quadrilateral ABCDABCD has an incircle if and only if

AB+CD=BC+ADAB+CD=BC+AD

If the tangency points of the incircle are marked as above (P,Q,R,SP,Q,R,S on AB,BC,CD,DAAB,BC,CD,DA respectively) then prove that PR,QS,AC,BDPR,QS,AC,BD concur.

Finally prove that SP,BD,QRSP,BD,QR concur.

Lemma 7 pascal’s theorem

Prove that in any cyclic hexagon ABCDEFABCDEF, if we let P=AF∩CDP=AF∩CD, Q=AB∩DEQ=AB∩DE and R=BC∩EFR=BC∩EF (intersections of opposite sides) then PQRPQR are collinear.

Lemma 8 pivot theorem

In a triangle ABCABC, A′,B′,C′A′,B′,C′ lie on lines (not necessarily internal) BC,CA,ABBC,CA,AB respectively. Prove that the circumcircles of AB′C′,BC′A′,CA′B′AB′C′,BC′A′,CA′B′ pass through a common point.

Lemma 9 miquel point

Prove that for any four lines, there exists a fixed point PP, such that the circumcircle of any triangle formed by three of the four lines goes through PP.

Lemma 10 miquel point of cyclic quadrilateral

In a convex cyclic quadrilateral ABCDABCD with circumcenter OO, AB∩CD=PAB∩CD=P, BC∩DA=QBC∩DA=Q, AC∩BD=RAC∩BD=R. Prove OPQROPQR are orthocentric (that is, the orthocenter of any three of them is the fourth).

Furthermore, let MM be the miquel point of lines AB,BC,CD,DAAB,BC,CD,DA. Prove that ORMORM are collinear.
