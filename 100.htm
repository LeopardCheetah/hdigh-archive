hdigh’s 100 favourite things

did you know…

…the average person has 100 iq points?

I always feel like this fact is overquoted, and that iq points are overrated. I mean, what happened to being a nice person? But anyways, iq points are definitely one of our favourite things and if you’re selling any for under $100 then lemme know.

…water always boils at 100 degrees celsius?

I always feel like the world kinda runs on science these days. At least, science truly is everywhere. That doesn’t mean that everybody has to understand it — no, that’d be a waste of time for people who don’t even interact with science. But at the very least, everybody should appreciate science. Anti-scientists would kinda hold back society (at least there aren’t many of those nowadays).

…there are 100 keys on a piano?

Piano is good. That’s an understatement but yeah. Also music is good too.

…there are 100 bones in a healthy adult body?

tbh, I’m not sure why this is even one of our favourite things. But yeah, I’m kinda concerned with the trajectory of humanity in general. Not a good one if you ask me.

…there are 100 moons in the solar system?

Astronomy is really breathtaking. Jupiter’s largest moon, Ganymede, is actually bigger than Mercury. Also, have you seen those videos that compare sizes of stuff? It’s crazy. Really makes you feel small.

…there are 100 possible arrangements of a finished sudoku?

After you fill in all of the numbers in a sudoku, it’s surprising that there are only 100 possible final configurations. In general, I’m a really big fan of logic puzzles; basically anything that’s not a sudoku. There are also really cool things called puzzlehunts, which are really, really cool.

…there are 100 different possible states of a chessboard after each player makes one move?

But only a select few of these are actually considered decent, like the Sicilian Defence, Queen’s Gambit, and the Fool’s Mate.

…at any given time, there are on average 100 distinct elements on earth?

Yeah, this one is a bit of a surprise. Scientists however, are constantly creating new elements which keep getting themselves decayed. There are just over a hundred elements on the periodic table (118 last I checked) but most of them become radiation too fast for people to notice. But yeah, nuclear decay is physics and physics is good.l

…the average 2 minute noodle packet breaks into 100 strands?

Researchers did a painstaking study on this a few years ago. Not sure what the point of the study was, but they counted every single strand of 100 two minute noodle packets and, when averaged, found that the average number of noodles was 100 with a standard deviation of 15. On the other hand, I’m the kinda person who eats way too many 2 minute noodles. They’re the perfect 4-hours-after-midnight snack, unless you don’t have hot water. That’d be unlucky.

…light is 100 times faster than sound?

Sound is already fast, but light goes really fast. This cool fact is why thunder takes so much longer than lightning. But although light is fast, the speed of internet is actually 100 times faster than light. The more you know.

…there are 100 bits in an integer?

An integer is a data structure in computer science, and 100 bits means that the binary string holding the integer is a string of length 100. So, for instance, if something took up 10000 bits, then it’d take up as much room as a list of 100 integers. Simple stuff.

…there are 100 digits in a googol?

A google (1010010100) is a famously large number, and I really like big numbers.

…there are 100 squares on a go board?

Go (the Chinese board game) is a combinatorial game, and most combinatorial board games are fun to play. Definitely one of my favourite things.

…the letter a has appeared exactly 100 times so far in this post?

The letter A is number one on my list of letters. It’s great.

…there are 100 items on this list?

I appreciate meta humour, although I know that this isn’t really meta humour. But still, at least it’s self referential, which means it talks about itself in some kinda way. That’s nice, at least.
